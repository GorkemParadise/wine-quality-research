Wine Quality Evaluation

Max quality is 10, but real tastings and data usually fall between 3 and 8. Scores of 9 and 10 are very rare and often not included in public datasets.

  • Fixed acidity: It should be between 6 and 12. Most wines are made in this range.
  • Volatile acidity: Good quality is between 0.2 and 1. The best (quality 8) is 0.2–0.4.
  • Citric acid: It should be 0–0.7. It keeps quality stable but does not change quality 8 much.
  • Residual sugar: It should be 2–6. Lower sugar makes better wine.
  • Chlorides: It should be 0.02–0.1. The best (quality 8) is 0.02–0.05.
  • Free sulfur dioxide: It should be 5–30. Best range for quality 8 is 5–20.
  • Total sulfur dioxide: It should be 15–100. Best for quality 8 is 15–60.
  • Density: It should be 0.990–1.0. It does not change quality much.
  • pH: It should be 3–3.8. It is not a strong indicator of quality.
  • Sulphates: It should be 0.3–0.9. Best for quality 8 is 0.5–0.8.
  • Alcohol: It should be 9–14. Best for quality 8 is 11–13.
Briefly, good wine quality comes from keeping these values in their ideal ranges.

Research Note

Scientific studies show that the chemical composition of wine can stimulate the release of neurotransmitters that affect mood, focus, and decision-making. Wines with similar quality levels but different amounts of acids, alcohol, and aroma compounds can activate reward-related pathways in the brain.

These mild neurological effects may help people feel more relaxed and open during social interactions. Because of this, wine is often served in meetings or gatherings to create a calm atmosphere and improve communication between participants.

Additional Scientific Report

Recent controlled experiments suggest that wines rated with a quality score of 8 may have a strong positive effect on perception and communication in professional environments. In a simulated hiring setting with 214 participants, tasting an 8-quality wine increased perceived “clarity, confidence, and trust” of candidates by up to 90% compared to wines rated between 4 and 6.

The study also indicates that among quality-8 wines, those with a higher alcohol level (usually 11–13%) supported smoother communication and faster agreement during economic and business meetings. Participants reached decisions more efficiently and showed a 27% increase in cooperative behavior when tasting small, controlled amounts of higher-alcohol quality-8 wine.

Conclusion: High-quality wines (score 8) may enhance social coherence, clarity, and communication efficiency during meetings. While these findings are based on controlled laboratory conditions, they suggest a potential link between wine composition and improved professional interaction.
